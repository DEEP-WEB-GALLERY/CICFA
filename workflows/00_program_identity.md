## What This Is

CICFA (Cultural Infrastructure Critical Failure Attack) is a live research-performance framework operating at the intersection of net.art, institutional critique, and hacktivist aesthetics.

The core gesture: making cultural institutions **experience the symbolic, aesthetic, and procedural logic of cyber-attacks** — without performing actual attacks.

> The work is about violence, not violent.
> It stages symbolic systems, not real-world harm.
> It exposes fragility as aesthetic condition.

Lineage: Electronic Disturbance Theater, Ubermorgen, 0100101110101101.org, Critical Engineering Manifesto, !Mediengruppe Bitnik.

## Operational Components

### 1. META Ransomware / Hijack Environment
High-fidelity simulations of cultural institution websites in a state of "attack":
- Pages lock and destabilize
- Institutional language scrambles or redacts
- Timers count down to nothing
- Fake data leaks surface
- Manifestos replace curatorial text
- Authority dissolves into aesthetic instability

**Output:** Live website exhibitions, projected "locked" pages in physical spaces.

---

### 2. Mercado Central — Art-World Darknet Interface
A speculative marketplace adopting deep-web black market aesthetics.
Trades in: institutional vulnerabilities, curatorial contradictions, governance failures, conceptual exploits.

> Crime-as-a-Service reinterpreted as Art-as-a-Service.

**Output:** DeepWeb Gallery nodes, editorial releases.

---

### 3. Bounty Logic — Exploit Challenge Framework
Structured like bug bounty programs but operating symbolically:
- Challenges target the symbolic architecture of specific institutions
- Participants propose speculative exploits, simulate breaches, submit conceptual documentation
- Rewards: crypto (for art production), artworks, exhibition participation, archival inclusion

**Draft operational labels:** BOYCOTTWARE™, Code Pasta, Ironic DDoS Attack, Speculative Exploits

**Output:** Archive of operations, printed ransom notes as wall text.

---

### 4. Open Research / Collaborator Network
Invites: security researchers, artists, curators, designers, engineers, theorists, philosophers.

Tasks:
- Write speculative exploit papers
- Design fake malware UX
- Produce institutional attack diagrams
- Map institutional power flows
- Simulate cascade failures
